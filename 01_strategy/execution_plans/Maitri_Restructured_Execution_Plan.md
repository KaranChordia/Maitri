# Maitri Dolls - Restructured Execution Plan

This plan is reorganized around what Shwethika explicitly wants help with:

1. Building presence before launch.
2. Creating strategic brand and story assets.
3. Validating demand before investing heavily in inventory.

## 1. Core Strategic Interpretation

Shwethika does not only need marketing support. She needs Maitri to become a story-led brand ecosystem before the physical dolls are ready.

The immediate job is not to sell dolls yet. The immediate job is to make parents, children, educators, and early supporters care about the characters.

Recommended strategic frame:

Maitri should launch first as a character and storytelling universe, then convert that community into waitlist interest, beta readers, school pilots, and eventually preorder demand.

## 2. The Workstreams

The full project should be structured into eight workstreams.

1. Brand Positioning Strategy
2. Character Universe Bible
3. Pre-Launch Community Roadmap
4. Social Media Strategy for 6 Months
5. Story Newsletter System
6. Parent Community and Beta Reader System
7. School Workshop and Historical Storytelling System
8. Waitlist, Preorder, and Demand Validation Roadmap

These workstreams are connected, but they should not all be built at the same depth immediately. The first 30 days should focus on positioning, Manu, waitlist setup, and content testing.

# Workstream 1 - Brand Positioning Strategy

## Objective

Define what Maitri stands for, who it serves, why it matters, and how it differs from a normal doll brand.

## Recommended Positioning

Maitri is a story-first companion doll universe inspired by remarkable women from India's history and present day. Each character helps children discover courage, curiosity, kindness, resilience, and leadership through play, storytelling, and cultural belonging.

## Brand Promise

Every Maitri friend helps a child discover the hero within.

## Brand Differentiation

Maitri is not only a doll.

It is:

- A companion.
- A storybook character.
- A cultural memory keeper.
- A parent-child conversation starter.
- A school workshop tool.
- A values-led play experience.

## Key Audiences

### Primary Audience

Parents of children aged roughly 5-10 who want meaningful, culturally rooted, premium play.

### Secondary Audience

Grandparents, relatives, and NRI families looking for Indian identity-led gifts.

### Institutional Audience

Schools, educators, libraries, museums, book clubs, and parent communities.

## Brand Pillars

1. Friendship: Maitri means friendship. The doll must feel like a trusted companion.
2. Courage: Courage should be shown through everyday choices, not only grand heroic acts.
3. Representation: Indian children should see familiar faces, skin tones, hair, clothes, regions, and values.
4. Story: The story should create emotional attachment before the doll is revealed.
5. Learning Through Play: Every doll should support imagination, empathy, reflection, and parent-child conversation.

## Tone of Voice

Warm, brave, culturally rooted, emotionally intelligent, child-friendly, and parent-trusted.

# Workstream 2 - Character Universe Bible

## Objective

Create a repeatable system for developing every Maitri character so the brand universe stays consistent.

## Why This Matters

If Maitri grows beyond one doll, it needs rules. The universe bible prevents each character from feeling random or disconnected.

## Character Bible Template

Each character should include:

- Character name
- Historical inspiration
- Era
- Region
- Age depiction in story
- Core value
- Emotional struggle
- Brave choice
- Signature object
- Visual palette
- Clothing references
- Hair and feature notes
- Language or regional words
- Key child takeaway
- Parent conversation prompt
- Activities for child and doll
- Historical facts
- Fictionalized story elements
- Sensitivity notes
- Illustration direction
- Social media content hooks

## Manu Starter Position

Manu should be the first universe anchor.

Core message:

Before she was Rani Laxmibai, she was a girl who asked why.

Value:

Courage.

Child takeaway:

Courage begins before you grow up.

Content hook:

What would Manu do?

# Workstream 3 - Pre-Launch Community-Building Roadmap

## Objective

Build a community before the dolls are available, using storytelling and participation.

## Community Strategy

Maitri should not ask people to simply follow or wait. It should invite them into the world.

Recommended community name:

Maitri Circle

## Community Segments

- Parents
- Children and families
- Beta readers
- Educators
- Schools
- Cultural storytellers
- Early supporters and founder customers

## Community-Building Phases

### Phase 1: Discovery and Soft Launch

Timing: Month 1

Goals:

- Introduce Maitri's mission.
- Introduce Manu as a character.
- Build waitlist.
- Recruit beta readers.
- Test which story hooks resonate.

### Phase 2: Story Attachment

Timing: Months 2-3

Goals:

- Release story snippets.
- Share parent-child prompts.
- Run beta reading sessions.
- Start newsletters.
- Build repeat engagement.

### Phase 3: Participation

Timing: Months 3-4

Goals:

- Let parents and children respond to stories.
- Run polls, questions, and character guessing games.
- Invite schools for pilot workshops.
- Collect testimonials and feedback.

### Phase 4: Product Trust

Timing: Months 4-5

Goals:

- Share doll development journey.
- Explain safety and materials.
- Show prototype details carefully.
- Build confidence before preorder.

### Phase 5: Conversion Readiness

Timing: Month 6

Goals:

- Open founder waitlist or priority reservation.
- Validate price and demand.
- Identify first buyers.
- Decide whether inventory investment is justified.

# Workstream 4 - Social Media Content Strategy for 6 Months

## Objective

Use Instagram and YouTube Shorts to make Maitri visible, emotionally resonant, and community-led before product launch.

## Primary Platforms

- Instagram
- YouTube Shorts

## Secondary Content Extensions

- Newsletter snippets
- WhatsApp parent community prompts
- School outreach material
- Website or landing page content

## Content Pillars

### 1. Character Reveals

Purpose:

Build curiosity around each Maitri heroine.

Examples:

- Before she was a queen, she was a girl who asked why.
- Guess the heroine from these three clues.
- Meet Manu: the girl with the big, brave heart.

### 2. Historical Storytelling Content

Purpose:

Make Indian history emotionally accessible for children and parents.

Examples:

- One childhood moment from an Indian heroine.
- One regional detail from Manu's world.
- One value from history that children can use today.

### 3. Parent-Facing Content

Purpose:

Give parents reasons to trust and join Maitri.

Examples:

- Why stories shape confidence.
- Why representation matters in play.
- How to talk to children about courage.

### 4. Child-Friendly Story Moments

Purpose:

Make children want to know the character.

Examples:

- Manu hears horses in the distance.
- Manu asks, "Why can't girls learn everything boys learn?"
- Manu learns that being brave does not mean never being scared.

### 5. Behind the Doll

Purpose:

Build product trust and anticipation.

Examples:

- Hair styling tests.
- Fabric and clothing inspiration.
- Doll safety decisions.
- Storybook illustration previews.

### 6. Community Participation

Purpose:

Make the audience feel involved.

Examples:

- Vote on a story cover line.
- Ask your child: what does bravery mean?
- Apply to become a beta reader.
- Which heroine should Maitri reveal next?

# Workstream 5 - Story Newsletters

## Objective

Use email to deepen attachment beyond fast social content.

## Newsletter Concept

Letters from Maitri

## Newsletter Types

1. Character Letter

A short letter written from the world of the character.

2. Parent Note

A thoughtful note for parents on values, confidence, representation, and storytelling.

3. Story Preview

A small excerpt from the book.

4. Activity Prompt

A parent-child or child-doll activity.

5. Behind-the-Scenes Note

Updates on illustration, doll design, safety, or production.

## Suggested Frequency

Twice per month at the start. Weekly only after engagement is proven.

# Workstream 6 - Parent Community and Beta Readers

## Objective

Create a feedback loop before product launch.

## Parent Community

Purpose:

- Understand parent needs.
- Build trust.
- Test messaging.
- Recruit early supporters.

Potential platforms:

- WhatsApp community
- Email group
- Instagram close friends or broadcast channel
- Private Facebook group if the audience uses it

## Beta Readers for Books

Beta readers should include:

- Parents of children in target age range.
- Children across different reading levels.
- Educators or librarians.
- Cultural or historical reviewers.

## Feedback To Capture

- Did the child enjoy the story?
- Which scene did they remember?
- Was anything confusing?
- Did the parent feel the content was age-appropriate?
- Would they want the doll after reading the story?
- What price range feels acceptable?
- Would they recommend Maitri to another parent?

# Workstream 7 - School Workshops and Historical Storytelling

## Objective

Turn Maitri into an educational and cultural experience, not just a consumer product.

## Workshop Format

Example workshop:

What Does Courage Look Like?

Character:

Manu

Age group:

6-9

Duration:

45-60 minutes

Structure:

1. Read one story scene.
2. Ask children what Manu felt.
3. Discuss one value: courage.
4. Activity: draw or write one brave thing they can do.
5. Take-home parent prompt.

## School Outreach Assets Needed

- One-page school introduction.
- Workshop deck.
- Teacher guide.
- Student worksheet.
- Parent take-home note.
- Feedback form.

## Historical Storytelling Guardrail

Every story should clearly separate:

- Verified historical facts.
- Child-friendly interpretation.
- Fictionalized scenes created for storytelling.

# Workstream 8 - Waitlist, Preorder, and Demand Validation

## Objective

Validate real demand before investing heavily in inventory.

## Waitlist Strategy

Do not position it as only "join the waitlist."

Recommended CTA:

Join the Maitri Circle for early character stories, parent-child activities, beta reader invitations, and first access to founder preorders.

## Waitlist Form Fields

- Parent name
- Email
- City and country
- Child age
- Buyer type: parent, grandparent, educator, gift buyer, NRI family
- Which matters most: representation, history, confidence, Indian culture, meaningful gifting, educational play
- Would you like to beta read stories?
- Would you like school workshop information?
- Expected price comfort range
- Preorder interest level

## Preorder Strategy

Because manufacturing and compliance are still uncertain, avoid full preorder payments until timelines are reliable.

Recommended stages:

1. Waitlist
2. Founder Circle
3. Preorder interest survey
4. Refundable reservation or priority deposit only if legal, operational, and fulfillment conditions are clear
5. Full preorder when production and certification confidence is high

## Demand Validation Signals

Track:

- Waitlist conversion rate
- Email open rate
- Email click rate
- Beta reader applications
- Parent survey completions
- Workshop inquiries
- Price comfort range
- Preorder intent
- Character preference
- Child response to stories
- Parent objections

## Inventory Decision Gate

Before investing heavily in inventory, Maitri should ideally have:

- A meaningful waitlist.
- Strong story engagement.
- Clear parent willingness to pay.
- Beta-reader proof that children connect with the characters.
- Workshop or institutional interest.
- Clarity on compliance and production timelines.
