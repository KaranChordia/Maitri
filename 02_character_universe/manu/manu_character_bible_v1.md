## 1. Character Snapshot

- Character name: Manu.
- Historical inspiration: Manikarnika Tambe, later remembered as Rani Laxmibai of Jhansi.
- Era: childhood in Bithoor, early-to-mid 1800s.
- Region: born in Varanasi; childhood setting associated with Bithoor on the banks of the Ganga.
- Age depiction in Maitri Book 1: around 10.
- Wider age range for childhood stories: 7-10.
- Core value: courage with tenderness.
- Secondary values: curiosity, fairness, patience, friendship, and emotional steadiness.
- Inner question: What does it mean to do what is right, even when it is hard?
- One-line character promise: Manu helps children see that courage can begin before they feel ready.
- Child takeaway: You do not have to be grown up to be brave.
- Parent takeaway: Manu gives families a warm way to talk about courage, fairness, patience, and trying again.
- Character reveal hook: Before she was a queen, she was a girl who asked why.

## 3. Emotional Arc

### What Manu Wants

Manu wants to learn fully, ask questions freely, ride, practice, belong, and be taken seriously without losing her warmth.

### What Manu Fears

Manu fears being told that her world must stay small. She also feels the ordinary fear of trying something difficult before she knows she can do it.

### What Feels Unfair

Manu notices that some people expect girls to stay quiet, learn less, or stand aside while boys are allowed to train, ride, question, and lead.

### Brave Choice

Manu asks why, tries anyway, chooses care before showing off, and stays kind even when others doubt her.

### Key Turning Point

In Book 1, Manu does not "defeat" the difficult horse. She learns to notice, wait, listen, and earn trust. Courage becomes patience in motion.

### What Manu Learns

Manu learns that courage is not only public and loud. It can be quiet, patient, kind, curious, and steady.

### How Children Can Relate

Children can relate to:

- wanting to try something adults or older children think is too hard
- feeling nervous before the first attempt
- asking why something is unfair
- learning that bravery includes kindness
- taking one small step instead of needing to be fearless

## 4. Four Courages Of Manu

Use this as Manu's repeatable values system across story, website, activities, and content.

### Asking Courage

Manu asks "why" when something feels unfair.

Child prompt: What is one brave question you can ask kindly?

### Trying Courage

Manu begins even when the first step feels hard.

Child prompt: What is one thing you can try once this week?

### Kindness Courage

Manu chooses friendship and care before proving herself.

Child prompt: Who could use a kind friend today?

### Quiet Courage

Manu stays close on hard days and does what is right even when no one is watching.

Child prompt: What small right thing can you do today?

## 6. Visual Identity

### Palette

- golden sunrise
- Ganga river blue
- marigold yellow
- warm terracotta
- palace stone
- soft lamp light
- mango-leaf green as an accent

### Clothing Direction

Use bright childhood clothing that feels rooted but active: lehenga, kurta-style forms, scarf or sash, and practical movement for riding and play.

Avoid heavy royal styling in Book 1. Manu should feel like a child the reader can befriend, not a distant queen.

### Hair And Features

- dark hair often escaping its braid
- expressive, serious, warm eyes
- determined but emotionally open face
- posture that shifts from curiosity to focus to joy

### Expression And Posture

Manu should often appear:

- leaning forward with curiosity
- pausing before trying
- looking closely rather than rushing
- smiling after effort, not only after winning
- gentle with Badal

### Material And Texture References

- cotton
- soft woven fabrics
- brass accents
- painted wood
- stone courtyard surfaces
- paper letters
- marigold garlands

### Doll Design Notes

- The doll should feel beautiful, premium, and playable.
- Styling should distinguish story richness from child-safe production design.
- Delicate jewelry, small detachable accessories, and ornate trims need safety review before product claims.
- Book 1 should support a less battle-heavy outfit and a more active childhood look.

### Illustration Notes

- Warm, cinematic, storybook-like, and emotionally readable.
- Avoid overly fierce warrior poses in the first story.
- Let courage show through attention, movement, and expression.

## 7. Voice And Behavior

### How Manu Speaks

Manu speaks with curiosity, directness, warmth, and a little spark. She asks real questions. She does not sound like a slogan.

### Words And Phrases She Might Use

- "Why not?"
- "Let me try."
- "Maybe she is scared too."
- "One step first."
- "I can be brave and kind."
- "The ripples will last."

### When Afraid

Manu breathes, watches carefully, asks for one chance, and begins with a small action.

### When Curious

Manu asks questions, notices details, follows sounds or movement, and wants to understand before judging.

### With Friends

Manu challenges unfairness, but she does not humiliate others. She invites, includes, and repairs.

### What Manu Should Not Do

- She should not sound like an adult motivational speaker.
- She should not solve every problem by fighting.
- She should not mock fear.
- She should not be perfect.
- She should not be used to make unsafe product, history, or preorder claims.

## 9. Content System

### Reusable Character Reveal Copy

Meet Manu, Maitri's first brave friend.

Before she became Rani Laxmibai of Jhansi, Manu was a girl who asked why, got back up, learned with joy, and chose kindness even when it was hard.

Her story helps children see courage in everyday moments: asking a question, trying again, staying close on sad days, and doing what is right.

### Parent-Facing Explanation

Manu helps parents talk to children about courage without making courage feel harsh or impossible. Her first story keeps bravery close to childhood: patience, fairness, friendship, and trying again.

### Child-Facing Prompt

What would Manu do if something felt hard, unfair, or scary?
