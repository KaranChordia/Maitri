# Letters From Maitri: Month 1 Drafts

## Letter 1: Meet Manu

Subject: Meet Manu, Maitri's first brave friend

Dear Maitri Circle,

Before she was remembered as the Rani of Jhansi, she was Manu: a curious girl who asked why, learned to ride, tried hard things, and believed courage could begin in childhood.

Maitri begins with her because children should meet bravery in a form they can understand. Not only as battle or greatness, but as a question, a first try, a kind choice, and a steady heart.

This month, we are building Manu's story world and inviting early families to help us understand what children connect with most.

Prompt for home: What is one brave thing your child did this week?

## Letter 2: The Girl Who Asked Why

Subject: A question can be the start of courage

Dear Maitri Circle,

In Manu's story, one of her first brave acts is simple: she asks why.

Why should boys learn things girls cannot? Why should a child stay quiet when something feels unfair? Why should courage wait until adulthood?

For children, questions are not interruptions. They are the beginning of thought, confidence, and self-respect.

Prompt for home: Ask your child, "What is one thing you wonder about?" Then listen without correcting too quickly.

## Letter 3: Courage Can Be Kind

Subject: Manu's softer kind of bravery

Dear Maitri Circle,

One of the things we love most about Manu is that her courage is not only fierce. It is also tender.

She practices, she races, she stands tall. But she also learns that bravery can mean being the first one to be kind, sitting beside someone on a hard day, and doing something loving when you feel helpless.

That is the kind of courage children can use every day.

Prompt for home: Who might need a quiet, kind friend today?

## Letter 4: The Ripples That Last

Subject: Small brave choices can leave ripples

Dear Maitri Circle,

Near the end of Manu's childhood story, she watches ripples spread across the river and thinks about what it means to do the right thing.

Children do not need to solve the whole world to feel powerful. They need to see that small choices matter: telling the truth, trying again, helping someone, asking a question, or standing beside a friend.

That is what Maitri stories are meant to create: little ripples of courage.

Prompt for home: What small right thing can your family do this week?
