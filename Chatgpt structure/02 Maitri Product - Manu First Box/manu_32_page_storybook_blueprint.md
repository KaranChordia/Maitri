# Manu 32-Page Storybook Blueprint

Working title: **Manu: The Horse Nobody Could Ride**

## Story Intent

Book 1 should not tell Manu's whole life.

It should focus on one defining childhood period: a difficult horse arrives, nobody can ride it, and Manu learns that courage is not force. Courage is patience, attention, and one step forward when you feel afraid.

The book should end before Manu becomes queen.

Why:

- Children relate to children.
- The emotional arc stays focused.
- The ending creates appetite for Book 2.
- Maitri can build a longer character ladder without rushing into war or heavy historical material.

## Story Section - 22 Pages

### Act 1 - Manu Feels Different

Pages: 1-5

Purpose:

Show Manu as a child who notices unfair rules and wants more from the world.

Beats:

- Manu watches boys train, ride, or practice skills.
- Someone implies girls should not do the same things.
- Manu does not respond with anger first. She asks, "Why not?"
- Her father or mentor sees her curiosity.
- A difficult horse is mentioned or arrives.

Child takeaway:

It is okay to ask brave questions.

### Act 2 - The Horse Nobody Could Ride

Pages: 6-11

Purpose:

Introduce the horse as the story challenge and mirror Manu's inner struggle.

Beats:

- The horse is strong, frightened, and misunderstood.
- Adults or older children call the horse impossible.
- Manu notices something others miss.
- She approaches with patience instead of showing off.
- The horse does not trust her immediately.

Child takeaway:

Courage can be quiet and patient.

### Act 3 - Manu Must Prove Herself

Pages: 12-17

Purpose:

Create stakes without making the story harsh or violent.

Beats:

- The horse may be sold or taken away because nobody can handle it.
- Manu asks for one chance.
- Others doubt her.
- She feels afraid but remembers what she has learned.
- She chooses care before control.

Child takeaway:

Being brave does not mean being fearless.

### Act 4 - Courage And Patience Win

Pages: 18-22

Purpose:

Resolve the story with emotional satisfaction and a future-facing line.

Beats:

- Manu earns the horse's trust.
- She rides or leads the horse safely.
- The community sees her differently.
- Manu feels proud, but also grateful to the horse.
- The Peshwa or elder says a line that points forward: one day, this girl will change history.

Child takeaway:

Real courage helps others feel safe too.

## Letter From Manu - 2 Pages

Purpose:

Make Manu feel like a friend speaking directly to the child.

Message:

- People may tell you what you can and cannot do.
- You can ask brave questions.
- Courage is not being fearless.
- Courage is taking one step forward even when you are scared.

Drafting rule:

Keep the language intimate, simple, and quotable. This section may become the page parents remember most.

## Activity Pages - 6 Pages

### Activity 1 - Design Your Warrior Crest

Child creates a personal crest using stickers and drawing.

Value:

Identity and self-expression.

### Activity 2 - What Would Manu Do?

Child chooses what Manu might do in simple scenarios.

Value:

Decision-making and fairness.

### Activity 3 - Courage Tracker

Seven-day tracker for one small brave action per day.

Value:

Everyday courage.

### Activity 4 - Spot The Difference

Horse stable scene.

Value:

Observation and playful re-entry into the story world.

### Activity 5 - Jhansi Maze

Help Manu reach Badal.

Value:

Persistence and problem solving.

### Activity 6 - My Brave Promise

Prompt:

I promise to be brave when...

Value:

Reflection and parent-child conversation.
