# Manu Story Content Set v1

## Polished Intro Excerpt

Before she was known as Rani Laxmibai of Jhansi, she was Manu: a curious girl with dust on her clothes, questions in her eyes, and a heart that did not know how to stay small.

Manu lived near the Ganga in Bithoor. She loved the sound of horses, the stories of heroes, and the feeling that the world was full of things she had not learned yet. When people said girls should be quiet, Manu wondered why. When people said something was too hard, Manu wanted to try.

One morning, she ran to the stables and asked her father to teach her to ride. The horse was tall. The ground looked far away. For one moment, Manu felt fear flutter inside her.

Then she sat up straight, took a deep breath, and held the reins.

That was one of Manu's first lessons: courage does not mean you are never afraid. Courage means you breathe, try, and begin anyway.

Maitri begins with Manu because her bravery is not only about battles or crowns. It is about questions, friendship, kindness, grief, practice, and doing what is right even when it is hard. She reminds every child that courage does not wait until you are grown up. It lives in you right now.

## Parent-Child Prompts

1. **Ask a brave question**
   - Manu asked why girls could not learn the same things boys learned.
   - Prompt: What is one question you have about something that feels unfair?

2. **Try the first step**
   - Manu felt nervous on the horse, but she began with one step.
   - Prompt: What is one hard thing you can try once this week?

3. **Be kind first**
   - Manu did not only prove herself; she also chose friendship.
   - Prompt: Who could use a kind friend today?

4. **Make ripples**
   - Manu believed the right thing could leave ripples.
   - Prompt: What small right thing can you do that might help someone else?

## Reusable Character Reveal Copy

Meet Manu, Maitri's first brave friend.

Her story helps children see courage in everyday moments: asking a question, trying again, staying close on sad days, and doing what is right.
