# Maitri Dolls - Working Strategy Brief

Prepared from Shwethika's email, the supplied Manu story document, the doll sample image, and reference scans on American Girl and Paola Reina.

## 1. Current Read

Maitri is not just a doll product. It is a story-led character universe built around Indian girls, Indian history, and values that children can internalize through play.

The strongest line from the email is this:

> "What I would like to do is have people fall in love with the characters long before they've ever seen the doll."

That should become the operating principle for the pre-launch phase.

## 2. Brand Repositioning

### Current Category Frame

18-inch Indian companion dolls inspired by remarkable women from India's history and present day.

### Stronger Category Frame

A story-first companion universe where children befriend brave Indian heroines before they own the doll.

### Recommended Positioning Statement

Maitri creates story-rich Indian companion dolls that help children discover courage, kindness, curiosity, and leadership through the lives of remarkable women from India's past and present.

### One-Line Brand Promise

A friend who helps every child find the hero within.

### Tagline Directions

- Friends with courage.
- Her story. Your courage.
- India's heroines, your child's companions.
- Every child deserves a brave friend.
- Stories to grow up with.

### Brand Pillars

- Friendship: The doll is a companion, not a collectible first.
- Representation: Children see Indian faces, skin tones, hair, clothing, languages, regions, and family contexts.
- Courage With Tenderness: The stories should not flatten bravery into only battle or achievement. Manu's story already does this well by showing riding, grief, friendship, and kindness.
- History Made Personal: The child should feel, "This story belongs to me too."
- Play With Purpose: Storybooks, worksheets, dressing, role-play, school workshops, and family conversations should all reinforce the same character values.

## 3. Competitive Reference Insights

### American Girl

American Girl's useful model is not only the doll. It is the ecosystem: 18-inch dolls, companion books, clothing, accessories, age segmentation, modern and historical worlds, and character encyclopedias. Their historical characters are explicitly framed around teaching girls about history and who they want to be.

Maitri should borrow the ecosystem logic, not the American tone. Maitri's differentiation should be Indian girlhood, Indian historical memory, regional diversity, and parent-trusted values.

### Paola Reina Manufacturing Reference

The Paola Reina reference points toward craft, material quality, hair, face sculpt, and production credibility. For Maitri, this matters because a premium doll must feel safe, tactile, and durable. The manufacturing story can later become trust content: how the doll is made, safety checks, materials, hair care, clothing details, and why parts were chosen.

## 4. Product Architecture

### Core Product

Each Maitri doll should include:

- 18-inch companion doll.
- Character storybook.
- Activity or reflection worksheets.
- Character card with values, region, language, timeline, and key question.
- Parent note explaining how to use the character in conversations.
- Care guide for hair, clothes, and safe play.

### Expansion Layers

- Outfit packs tied to story moments.
- Festival or regional accessories.
- Audio stories.
- Parent-child discussion cards.
- School workshop kits.
- Beta-reader book club.
- Character newsletters.
- Preorder collectible founder edition.

## 5. Audience Segments

### Primary Buyer

Urban and semi-urban Indian parents of children roughly ages 5-10 who want culturally rooted, meaningful, high-quality play.

### Secondary Buyer

Grandparents, aunts, uncles, and NRI families who want Indian identity gifts that feel premium and emotionally significant.

### Institutional Buyer

Schools, libraries, parent communities, book clubs, museums, cultural organizations, and workshop organizers.

### Important Note

The buyer is the parent, but the brand must win the child. The pre-launch content must therefore work at two levels:

- Parent level: representation, values, safety, quality, education.
- Child level: story, emotion, friendship, adventure, repeatable play.

## 7. Manu Story Assessment

The story document is a strong prototype because it does three important things:

- It begins with Manu as a child, not as a distant queen.
- It gives her a repeatable emotional lesson: courage can be asking why, trying again, being kind first, and staying close during grief.
- It ends with activities that convert reading into play and reflection.

Areas to tighten before publication:

- Historical review: confirm dates, names, events, and contested details with a historian or qualified reviewer.
- Age calibration: simplify sections if the target age is 5-7; preserve longer chapter style if target is 7-10.
- Sensitivity: avoid making violence the main aspiration; keep the emphasis on courage, fairness, responsibility, and compassion.
- Repeatable template: every book should end with activities, parent prompts, and a "real person" note.

## 8. Doll Sample Visual Read

The sample image feels premium, ornate, and culturally rich. It has a collectible quality.

Potential risk: if the final product is for children, loose jewelry, delicate trims, and small detachable accessories need safety review. The brand should distinguish between:

- Marketing concept imagery.
- Play-safe production doll.
- Collector or display edition.

This distinction matters for trust and compliance.

## 9. Six-Month Pre-Launch Roadmap

### Month 1 - Foundation

- Finalize positioning, audience, and brand voice.
- Create the character bible template.
- Select first 3-5 launch characters.
- Build landing page and waitlist.
- Start Instagram with story-first content.
- Recruit 20-30 parent beta readers.

### Month 2 - Manu Reveal Arc

- Publish Manu story snippets.
- Launch "What would Manu do?" reels and carousels.
- Share parent conversation prompts.
- Test two visual directions: heritage-premium vs warm-storybook.
- Collect waitlist reasons and objections.

### Month 3 - Community Validation

- Run parent survey.
- Run children story read-aloud sessions.
- Start newsletter: "Letters from Maitri."
- Create school workshop pilot deck.
- Measure willingness to preorder.

### Month 4 - Multi-Character Universe

- Reveal second and third characters without showing full dolls.
- Launch "Guess the heroine" content.
- Invite beta readers for each character.
- Start teacher and librarian outreach.

### Month 5 - Product Trust

- Share material, safety, hair, size, and care content.
- Explain compliance journey in parent-friendly language.
- Show prototype details only where safe and approved.
- Open founder waitlist tiers.

### Month 6 - Preorder Readiness

- Announce first production character.
- Launch founder edition interest list.
- Offer preorder only if compliance and supply timelines are credible.
- Run small paid test campaigns to validate acquisition cost.
- Confirm first 100-300 serious buyers before heavy inventory commitment.

## 10. Content Strategy Pillars

### 1. Character Storytelling

### 5. Community Participation

## 11. Waitlist And Preorder Strategy

### Waitlist Offer

Do not ask people only to "join the waitlist." Give them a reason.

Recommended offer:

Join the Maitri Circle to receive free character letters, early story chapters, parent activity prompts, and first access to founder preorders.

### Waitlist Segments

- Parent of child age 3-5.
- Parent of child age 6-8.
- Parent of child age 9-11.
- NRI family.
- School or educator.
- Gift buyer.

### Demand Signals To Track

- Email signup conversion rate.
- Story chapter open and click rates.
- Parent survey completion.
- Beta-reader applications.
- Preorder deposit interest.
- Price comfort range.
- Character preference.
- Objections: price, safety, age fit, historical accuracy, durability, delivery timeline.

### Preorder Guardrail

Avoid taking full preorder payment until compliance, production, and delivery timelines are reliable. A refundable founder deposit or priority reservation is safer if manufacturing remains uncertain.

## 12. Demand Validation Roadmap

### Low-Cost Validation

- Landing page with waitlist.
- Parent survey.
- Story sample downloads.
- Instagram content tests.
- WhatsApp parent group feedback.
- Beta read-aloud sessions.
- School workshop pilots.

### Medium-Cost Validation

- 3D renders or prototype photography.
- Paid Instagram tests.
- Small batch storybook print.
- Workshop-in-a-box pilot.
- Founder circle event.

### High-Cost Actions To Delay

- Large inventory order.
- Complex accessory SKUs.
- Multiple dolls at launch.
- Full app build.
- Heavy paid media before conversion proof.

## 15. Key Strategic Recommendation

Do not launch Maitri as a doll brand waiting for inventory.

Launch it now as a character and story community. Let the audience fall in love with Manu and the Maitri universe first. Use that love to validate demand, refine the product, and reduce inventory risk.
