# Launch Decision - Start With One Complete Doll Box

Date: 2026-06-15

## Decision

Maitri should launch with one complete first-box experience before scaling into a broader ecosystem.

The first box should prove two things:

1. Parents understand the doll as educational, meaningful, culturally rooted, and worth paying for.
2. Children form an emotional bond with the character and want to keep playing in her world.

## Why This Is The Right Move

Shwetika's approach is strategically sound because it reduces launch complexity while preserving perceived value.

Maitri does not need to launch with every future revenue stream. It needs one lovable character, one trustworthy parent-facing promise, and one box that feels complete. If that works, accessories, playsets, journals, games, workshops, and future books become natural extensions.

## Core Insight

The first product is not just a doll.

It is a child-character relationship packaged inside a parent-trusted learning experience.

That means the launch box must carry three jobs:

- Product job: the doll must feel beautiful, durable, safe, and giftable.
- Story job: the child must care about Manu as a friend.
- Parent job: the parent must see values, learning, and cultural meaning.

## First Launch Scope

In the first box:

- Doll.
- 32-page storybook.
- Letter from Manu.
- Six activity pages.
- Sticker pages.

Not in the first box:

- Large playsets.
- Multiple character lines.
- Full book series.
- Paid school programs.
- Heavy inventory variants.
- Complex app or AI experiences.

These should remain planned revenue layers, not day-one dependencies.

## Manu Story Direction

Recommended Book 1:

**Manu: The Horse Nobody Could Ride**

The story should focus on Manu as a child, before she becomes queen. This makes her emotionally accessible to children and gives the brand room to build future books.

The strongest story promise:

Before Manu changed history, she learned to be brave one small step at a time.

## Risks To Manage

- Do not overpack the first product until manufacturing cost and pricing are clear.
- Do not let accessories distract from the first child-character bond.
- Do not turn Manu into a distant heroic figure too early.
- Do not make public safety, material, compliance, delivery, or preorder claims until reviewed.
- Do not treat the 32-page book as filler. It is the emotional engine of the product.

## Practical Next Decisions

1. Confirm target age band for the first Manu box.
2. Confirm desired price band and cost ceiling.
3. Turn the 32-page storybook blueprint into a page-by-page draft.
4. Decide which stickers belong in-box versus later sticker packs.
5. Define one parent-facing validation test: would this box feel educational and gift-worthy?
6. Define one child-facing validation test: does the child ask to hear more about Manu?
