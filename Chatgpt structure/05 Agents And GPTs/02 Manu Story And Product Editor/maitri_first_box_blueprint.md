# Maitri First Doll Box Blueprint

## Box Objective

The first Maitri box should create a complete experience without trying to launch the entire Maitri ecosystem.

It must answer two questions:

- Parent question: Is this meaningful, educational, premium, and culturally rooted?
- Child question: Do I love this character and want to play with her again?

## In-Box Formula

Every first-launch Maitri box should include:

1. Doll.
2. 32-page character storybook.
3. Letter from character.
4. Six activity pages.
5. Sticker pages.

## Why This Works

The doll creates the emotional object.

The storybook creates the relationship.

The letter makes the doll feel like a friend.

The activities convert the story into parent-child reflection and repeat play.

The stickers create high perceived value at controlled cost.

## Manu Box Direction

First box character:

**Manu**

First story:

**The Horse Nobody Could Ride**

Core value:

**Courage with patience**

Core child promise:

You do not have to be grown up to be brave.

Core parent promise:

This story helps children talk about courage, fairness, patience, and trying again.

## Box Contents

### 1. Doll

Role:

The hero product and play companion.

Needs:

- Beautiful but play-safe styling.
- Durable clothes and hair.
- Clear distinction between prototype decoration and child-safe production design.
- Parent-friendly safety and care explanation when claims are approved.

### 2. 32-Page Storybook

Role:

The emotional engine of the product.

Recommended split:

| Section | Pages |
| --- | ---: |
| Story | 22 |
| Letter from Manu | 2 |
| Activities | 6 |
| Stickers | 2 |
| Total | 32 |

### 3. Letter From Manu

Role:

Make the doll feel like a friend, not only a product.

Tone:

Warm, direct, brave, emotionally simple.

The letter should speak to the child personally and give parents a quotable emotional moment.

### 4. Activity Pages

Role:

Make the book usable after the story is finished.

Rules:

- No extra materials required.
- Activities should work with pencil, crayons, and stickers.
- Each activity should connect to one story value.
- Avoid school-test energy. Keep the activities reflective and playful.

### 5. Sticker Pages

Role:

Increase perceived value and support repeat play.

Sticker categories:

- Characters.
- Objects.
- Values.
- Decorative motifs.

## What Should Stay Out Of The First Box

Keep these as later add-ons:

- Extra outfits.
- Warrior kit.
- Horse figure.
- Fort playset.
- Additional activity books.
- Flash cards.
- Journals.
- Map game.
- Multiple books.

## Validation Tests

Before scaling, test the first box with families:

### Parent Test

Ask:

- Does this feel educational?
- Does this feel culturally meaningful?
- Would you gift it?
- What price range feels reasonable?
- What safety, material, or quality questions do you have?

### Child Test

Observe:

- Does the child remember Manu's name?
- Does the child ask what happens next?
- Does the child use the stickers or activities without heavy instruction?
- Does the child want to play with the doll after hearing the story?

## Open Decisions

- Final target age band.
- Final doll size and material specification.
- First box price band.
- Manufacturing cost ceiling.
- Sticker count and size.
- Illustration style.
- Whether activity pages are inside the storybook or perforated.
- Whether the letter is printed inside the book or included as a separate insert.
