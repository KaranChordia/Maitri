# Manu Character Bible v1

## Character Identity

- Character name: Manu.
- Historical inspiration: Manikarnika Tambe, later Rani Laxmibai of Jhansi.
- Era: Childhood in Bithoor, early-to-mid 1800s.
- Region: Born in Varanasi; childhood setting in Bithoor on the banks of the Ganga.
- Age depiction in story: 7-10.
- Core value: Courage with tenderness.
- Inner question: What does it mean to do what is right, even when it is hard?

## Story Role

- Emotional struggle: Manu wants to learn, ride, question, and act in a world that expects girls to stay quiet.
- Brave choice: She asks why girls cannot learn everything boys learn, tries difficult skills, and chooses kindness even when others mock her.
- Key turning point: Manu wins the riding test and begins to understand that courage can be public and joyful, but later learns that courage can also be quiet love during grief.
- Child takeaway: Courage lives in everyday choices: asking a big question, trying again, being the first to be kind, staying close on hard days, and doing what is right.
- Parent conversation prompt: Where did you need courage today: in your body, your words, your kindness, or your patience?

## World Details

- Signature object: A small wooden sword and Badal the horse.
- Visual palette: Golden sunrise, Ganga river blue, marigold yellow, warm terracotta, palace stone, soft lamp light.
- Clothing references: Bright lehenga or kurta-style childhood clothing with practical movement for riding and play.
- Hair and feature notes: Dark hair often escaping its braid; bright serious eyes; expressive, warm, determined face.
- Language or regional words: Baba, Ganga, kheer, Jhansi.
- Cultural details: Ghats at dawn, palace courtyard, mango trees, oil lamp, river stones, sweet rice pudding, friendship around shared learning.

## Historical Handling

- Verified historical facts:
  - Manu is associated with Manikarnika Tambe, later Rani Laxmibai of Jhansi.
  - Her father was Moropant Tambe.
  - She is remembered for horse-riding and martial training.
  - Nana Sahib and Tatya Tope are historically associated with the 1857 uprising.
  - Rani Laxmibai became a major figure of resistance against the British East India Company.
- Fictionalized story elements:
  - Specific childhood dialogue.
  - Vikram as a child foil/friend.
  - The exact riding test and golden bangle scene.
  - The kheer scene and specific river conversations.
- Claims needing citation:
