# Maitri Foundation Retainer Plan

## Retainer Intent

Shwethika has accepted the foundation retainer direction. The first 90 days should stay narrow and deliberate: prove interest through the website, build the Manu-led character universe, and establish Maitri's Instagram and YouTube Shorts presence.

This phase should not behave like a full launch. It should create evidence, story attachment, and a repeatable content rhythm before heavier product, school, workshop, preorder, or AI workflow work begins.

## 90-Day Priorities

1. **Website interest capture**
   - Move from a demo waitlist to a validation surface.
   - Capture audience segment, child age, story interest, beta-reader interest, school interest, objections, and preorder signal.
   - Report monthly on quality of interest, not only signup count.

2. **Manu character universe**
   - Treat Manu as the first character anchor.
   - Complete the Manu character bible before expanding the universe.
   - Convert Manu's story into parent prompts, newsletters, social posts, and short video scripts.

3. **Instagram + YouTube launch**
   - Use Instagram as the design/caption anchor.
   - Reuse the same story beats as YouTube Shorts scripts.
   - Publish in a measured sequence: brand belief, Manu reveal, story moments, values prompts, and founder intent.

## Month 1 Operating Rhythm

### Week 1: Alignment + Website

- Confirm the public positioning: "A story-first companion universe where children befriend brave Indian heroines before they own the doll."
- Review the website and keep only pre-launch claims that are safe to publish.
- Finalize the waitlist + survey fields.
- Prepare the first-month content themes.

Deliverables:

- Live capture plan.
- Manu universe outline.
- Social launch calendar skeleton.

### Week 2: Character + Story Production

- Complete Manu's character bible v1.
- Create the first Manu intro excerpt.
- Draft parent-child prompts.
- Draft the first "Letters from Maitri" sequence.

Deliverables:

- Manu character bible v1.
- Manu intro story/excerpt.
- Parent prompts.
- Content batch 1.

### Week 3: Website + Social Launch

- Connect the website capture flow.
- Prepare Instagram and YouTube Shorts batch 2.
- Define weekly tracking fields.

Deliverables:

- Working capture flow.
- Social batch 2.
- Publishing checklist.

### Week 4: Reporting + Next Decisions

- Review website and content signals.
- Identify which audience segments are responding.
- Decide the next month's focus from evidence.

Deliverables:

- Monthly signal report.
- Content performance notes.
- Next-month roadmap.

## Month 2 Focus

- Improve website copy and survey questions based on month 1 responses.
- Expand Manu content into read-aloud, parent prompts, and beta-reader material.
- Continue Instagram + YouTube rhythm with stronger story excerpts.
- Begin beta-reader program structure only if story/beta interest is visible.

## Month 3 Focus

- Consolidate Manu as the first public character system.
- Prepare a second character only if Manu's story system is stable.
- Create a stronger monthly report for Shwethika: audience mix, content signals, objections, and recommended next decisions.
- Decide whether school/workshop, newsletter automation, or founder reservation language should enter months 4-6.

## Deferred To Months 4-6

- School/workshop one-pager unless educator interest appears early.
- Preorder or founder deposit language until product, compliance, pricing, and delivery assumptions are clearer.
- Broader AI workflows beyond content support and reporting.
- Full launch planning and inventory-facing communication.

## Review Guardrails

- Do not make public product safety, material, compliance, preorder, manufacturing, or delivery claims without Shwethika review.
- Separate verified historical facts from child-friendly interpretation and fictionalized scenes.
- Keep courage tender and child-safe: courage can be asking why, trying again, being kind first, staying close on hard days, and doing what is right.
- Measure quality of interest before pushing for sales.
