# Founder Note - First Box Launch Approach

Date added: 2026-06-15

## Source Summary

Shwetika's latest direction is to keep Maitri's first launch deliberately focused instead of trying to launch the full ecosystem on day one.

The core launch objective:

- Make parents feel the doll is educational and meaningful.
- Make children fall in love with the character.

The recommended first product is a complete but controlled box experience:

- Doll as the hero product.
- 32-page character storybook.
- 2-page letter from the character.
- 6 activity pages.
- 2 sticker pages.

For Manu, the first story should focus on one defining childhood period rather than her entire life. The proposed story frame is "The Horse Nobody Could Ride," centered on 10-year-old Manu. The book ends before she becomes queen so children can relate to her as a child and want the next story.

The suggested future Manu book ladder:

1. Manu: The Horse Nobody Could Ride.
2. Manu: Princess of Jhansi.
3. Manu: Defender of a Kingdom.
4. Manu: The Warrior Queen.

The commercial architecture separates the first box from later add-on products:

- Small add-ons: stickers, flash cards, activity books, journals, dress sets, hair accessories, mini pets.
- Play sets: Manu's Warrior Kit, Manu's Horse.
- Premium sets: Jhansi Fort Playset.
- Educational game: Build a Kingdom Map Game.

## Strategic Interpretation

This note confirms that Maitri's strongest immediate path is not a broad ecosystem launch. The first launch should prove that one character can create emotional attachment, educational trust, and repeat play.

The story should drive accessory demand, not the other way around.
